SUNDAY EDITION

The week you stopped counting

and started noticing.
BY THE EDITORS·6 MIN READ·WEEK 47

Monday began with rain on the window and oats on the stove. You walked the long way to the bus, the one through the park with the loud crows, and arrived to work with cold ears and a footprint of 1.18 kilograms — your lowest morning of the season.

Wednesday tested the experiment. A friend's birthday, a long table, a steak you didn't order but ate anyway. The day closed at 3.4. SAVANNA didn't scold; it simply noted, in the corner of the screen, that you were still on track for the week.

By Friday the grass on your hillside had filled back in. You repaired a pair of headphones instead of replacing them — a small, almost invisible act — and the grove in your dashboard grew by one tree.

The week ended at an average of 2.11 kg per day. Nineteen percent below the global target. The savanna, by all accounts, is doing well.
